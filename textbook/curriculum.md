# ArcWealth Draft Curriculum

Updated to match `ArcWealth Draft Curriculum.pdf`.

- Total modules: 5
- Total lessons: 59
- Estimated time: 4-6 hours
- Target audience: ages 15-18
- Lesson duration: 4-6 minutes

## Module 1: Financing Education

Goal: Understand the full cost of university and the main ways people pay for it.

1. What University Really Costs
2. Tuition, Living Costs, and Total Cost of Attendance
3. Comparing University Costs: EU, UK, USA, and East Asia
4. Hidden and One-Off Costs of Starting University
5. Family Support, Cultural Expectations, and the "Bank of Mom & Dad"
6. Scholarships, Grants, and Financial Aid
7. Student Loans: How They Work Across Different Regions
8. Part-Time Work, Work-Study, Side Hustles, and Entrepreneurship
9. Evaluating the Financial Return on Your Degree
10. Building a Personal University Funding Plan

## Module 2: Budgeting & Inflation

Goal: Teach students how to manage money and protect against rising costs.

1. Why Budgeting Matters
2. Income, Expenses, and Cash Flow
3. Needs, Wants, and Spending Priorities
4. Fixed Costs vs Variable Costs
5. Creating a Realistic Monthly Budget
6. Tracking Spending and Adjusting Your Plan
7. Managing Shared Costs and Roommate Finances
8. Emergency Funds and Contingency Planning
9. Insurance: What It Is and When You Need It
10. How Inflation Affects Your Budget and Purchasing Power
11. Student Tax Basics: Payslips, Filing, and Allowances

## Module 3: Banking & Fintech

Goal: Make students confident with the financial systems they use every day.

1. How Bank Accounts Work
2. Current Accounts vs Savings Accounts
3. Debit Cards: How They Work and When to Use Them
4. Credit Cards: What They Are and How They Work
5. Building Credit History as a Student
6. Credit Scores: What They Are and Why They Matter
7. Exchange Rates and Foreign Currency Spending
8. International Payments and Banking for Study Abroad
9. Mobile Banking, Digital Wallets, and Fintech Apps
10. Bank Fees, Charges, and Small Costs That Add Up
11. Financial Security: Scams, Fraud, and Protecting Your Accounts

## Module 4: Savings & Investing

Goal: Introduce the core ideas behind saving and growing wealth over time.

1. Why Saving Early Matters
2. Short-Term Savings vs Long-Term Goals
3. The Time Value of Money and Opportunity Cost
4. Simple Interest and Compound Interest
5. Interest Rates and Why They Matter
6. Inflation and How It Erodes Long-Term Purchasing Power
7. How Savings Accounts and Fixed Deposits Work
8. Saving vs Investing: Understanding the Difference
9. Risk and Return in Investing
10. What Stocks, Bonds, and Funds Are
11. Index Funds and Passive Investing
12. Diversification: Why It Reduces Risk
13. Long-Term Investing and Compounding Growth
14. Common Beginner Investing Mistakes
15. How Fees, Taxes, and Inflation Affect Investment Returns

## Module 5: Consumer Debt

Goal: Help students avoid expensive mistakes and use borrowing carefully.

1. How Borrowing and Interest Really Work
2. Good Debt vs Bad Debt
3. Credit Card Debt and Minimum Payments
4. Overdrafts and How They Add Up
5. Buy Now Pay Later and Short-Term Debt Traps
6. Payday Loans and Predatory Lending: What to Avoid
7. Student Debt vs Consumer Debt: Key Differences
8. Understanding Loan Terms for Large Purchases
9. Financing vs Saving vs Leasing: The Real Cost Comparison
10. Consumerism, Lifestyle Inflation, and Spending Pressure
11. What Happens If You Can't Pay: Default and Recovery
12. How to Make Smart Borrowing Decisions
